## PRESENTATION TIPS

### Before You Start:
1. Have demo loaded and ready (localhost:3000)
2. Clear browser cache for clean demo
3. Pre-ask AI question so it's in history
4. Have backup screenshots if live demo fails
5. Test microphone and screen share

### During Presentation:
1. **Smile and make eye contact** with judges
2. **Speak slowly and clearly** - you know the tech, they don't
3. **Show passion** - this solves a real problem
4. **Pause after key points** - let ideas sink in
5. **Watch the clock** - practice timing

### Demo Do's and Don'ts:
✅ DO: Show one complete user flow
✅ DO: Point out AI response speed
✅ DO: Highlight real-time chart updates
✅ DO: Show both chat and dashboard
❌ DON'T: Get lost in menus
❌ DON'T: Apologize for bugs
❌ DON'T: Speed through too fast

### Handling Q&A:
- **Listen completely** before answering
- **Repeat question** for whole audience
- **Be honest** about limitations
- **Bridge to strengths** - "Great question, and that connects to our scalability..."
- **Keep answers under 30 seconds**

**"Can this handle global markets?"**

## BODY LANGUAGE TIPS

✅ **Do:**
- Stand up if allowed (more energy)
- Use hand gestures (but not too much)
- Make eye contact with all judges
- Point to screen when showing features
- Nod when judges speak
- Lean forward slightly (shows engagement)

❌ **Don't:**
- Cross arms (looks defensive)
- Put hands in pockets (looks unprepared)
- Look at floor (shows lack of confidence)
- Fidget with pen/phone (distracting)
- Rush through nervousness (breathe!)
